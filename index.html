Conference paper: Dimensions

Abstract

            This article will guide you through the evolving concept of dimensions. We will begin by constructing our familiar world from the simplest possible elements, moving from a dimensionless point to the three-dimensional space we call home. From there, we will venture into the fourth dimension, exploring how Albert Einstein and Hermann Minkowski reshaped our understanding of reality by weaving space and time together into a single, dynamic fabric. We will take a leap into the abstract, using logic to imagine what higher spatial dimensions might be like. Finally, we will look at modern theories like string theory, which proposes that our universe contains hidden dimensions, not as a mathematical curiosity, but as a fundamental requirement for its existence.
